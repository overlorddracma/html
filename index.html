About me

My name is Liam. I am fifteen years old and I live in downtown Baltimore, Maryland. Although much of my time is spent with school-related assignments and commitments, I try to spend as much time as possible with my family, of which I am the oldest of four siblings.

Schedule permitting, I love to practice front-end web development, as well as sketchbook drawing and digital art. Many of my digital artworks are created using the app Procreate, and often my inspiration comes from school projects. I also enjoy creative writing, volleyball, caring for a plethora of succulents and air plants, obsessing over font choices, friendly debates, and playing/studying for Certamen, a quiz-bowl-styled Latin competition.

Coding education

I’ve taught myself coding since 2019, and my skills have transformed from simple python print statements to responsive web design. Although I have briefly used resources like freeCodeCamp and CodeAcademy, none have impacted me like Replit, which allows users to write and launch programs in dozens of programming languages. This is where I build all my projects. Using Replit, trial and error, extensive google queries, and many an hour of pondering, I’ve pushed my coding abilities to a level I wouldn’t have believed possible years ago.

Portfolio

The projects showcased below are written in HTML [Hyper-Text Markup Language], CSS [Cascading Style Sheets], and JS [JavaScript]. Note: Because I work exclusively with web design rather than backend scripts [which power many websites], few of my projects function the way one might expect. Many of these projects are designed as template websites for companies or newsletters as style examples without functionality. At the bottom of each project, I will denote whether the project is a template with the note: [template]. In addition, the denotation: [responsive] indicates that the website is visually styled to fit various screen sizes. If [non-responsive] is denoted, please try to open the link on a laptop. Otherwise, it may not look as intended.

Mathasaurus v3

This is math website that quizzes users on their multiplication math facts. I built it to help my younger brother, because at the time he had no interest in learning math facts. Let me explain the progression of Mathasaurus:
Version 1 was one of my first ventures into a project that relied on JavaScript, and the result was a annoying, cumbersome, and horrifyingly unwieldy saving system that ended up not working. I was still proud of it though.
I came back to it again, and I can't quite remember, but I think I added a timer and made some other minor edits. I'm pretty sure it was in v2 that I messed up my JS file and couldn't figure out how to fix it.
Then comes v3, where ended up with a MUCH neater JS file with MUCH less code. And, most importantly, this time I had experience with what I was working with. So here we are, v3!
